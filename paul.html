Paul Fox

Paul M. Fox is an experimental musician, sonic architect, and audio professional based in Dunedin, New Zealand. A foundational figure in the international space-rock and psychedelic underground, he contributed guitar and synthesizers to Farflung's seminal debut, 25,000 Feet Per Second, and was a core member of Nik Turner's Space Ritual, the ethno-techno project Anubian Lights, and the heavy psych/industrial outfit Pressurehed. His career is defined by high-profile collaborations and extensive touring, including a North American tour with The Brain (alongside Bond Bergland) supporting Cluster during their historic first visit to the US, and touring across the USA and Canada with Trash Can School. Additionally, he has collaborated live and in-studio with Helios Creed and alien-rock pioneers Chrome. Currently, he blends decades of analogue expertise with modern sound design for interactive media and game audio projects.
