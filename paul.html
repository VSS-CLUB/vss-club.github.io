Paul Fox

Paul M. Fox is an experimental musician, sonic architect, and audio professional based in Dunedin, New Zealand. A foundational figure in the international space-rock and psychedelic underground, he was a member of Farflung, appearing on their seminal debut 25,000 Feet Per Second and other early recordings. He was also a core member of Nik Turner's Space Ritual, the ethno-techno project Anubian Lights, and the heavy psych/industrial outfit Pressurehed. His career features high-profile collaborations and extensive touring, including a North American tour with The Brain (along with Bond Bergland) supporting Cluster during their historic first visit to the US, and touring across the USA and Canada with Trash Can School. Additionally, he has collaborated live and in-studio with Helios Creed and industrial-rock pioneers Chrome. Alongside his ongoing musical work, he applies decades of analogue expertise to sound design for film, game audio, and interactive media.
