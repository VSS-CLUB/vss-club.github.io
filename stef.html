Stef Animal

Stef is an experimental multi-instrumentalist, solo artist, and game designer based in Dunedin, New Zealand. She has been a fixture of the regional independent scene for over two decades, first gaining prominence with the long-running Dunedin fuzz-pop group Mëstar before co-founding the shoegaze ensemble The Golden Awesome, with whom she has toured internationally. Her solo work is defined by a "gadget-pop" aesthetic, most notably on the 2018 album Top Gear, which was composed entirely using obsolete 1980s computers and hacked electronic gear. Beyond her own projects, she has been a frequent collaborator and engineer within the Dunedin community, helping shape the sound of various artists on the Fishrider Records roster. Alongside her musical output, she works extensively in interactive media, frequently exploring the intersection of nostalgic lo-fi textures and sophisticated digital soundscapes.
